What is “Cochlea”?

The cochlea is our organ for hearing. It is a small structure roughly the size of a pea. This small organ is responsible for our sense of hearing.
The Cochlea is in the area called “the inner ear”.

Sound waves come into the outer ear, travels down the ear canal to hit the ear drum. Beyond the ear drum, we have small bones about the size of rice grain. These small bones pass sound vibration to the inner ear, which includes the cochlea and the balance organ.
The Cochlea is bony:

LEFT: A photograph of cochlea. The cochlea has hard bony shell that is fused to the skull. RIGHT: Bony shell of the cochlea was removed to expose the inside, by microdissection. Inside the cochlea, there are three fluid filled tube running in parallel to form a spiral.
