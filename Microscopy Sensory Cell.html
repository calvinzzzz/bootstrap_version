Sensory Cell

Sensory Hair Cells: Inside the Organ of Corti, there are very specialized cells called the auditory hair cells. These cells line up along the Organ of Corti.

Hairs!: As the name suggest, these hair cells have “hairs” on top of them – as seen in this microscopy image.

Hair Cells: Each hair cell is about 10 micrometers wide. Small hairs move when sound waves oscillate the cochlea. The hair bending causes hair cells to send out electrical signal to the nerve, telling the brain sound waves had hit them. LEFT: showing two hair cells. Green showing hair on top of the cell, blue showing cell nuclei, and red showing some protein inside the cell.

Sensory cells are tiny and important guys. In the human cochlea, we only have about 3200 of inner hair cells that can send electrical signals about sounds. We have another 12000 outer hair cells in the cochlea, that amplify the sound wave. Together, outer and inner hair cells make “hearing” possible.
