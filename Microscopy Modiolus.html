Lateral Wall & Modiolus

Modiolus:
The center of the cochlea spiral is called “the modiolus”. It is like the tree trunk, supporting the cochlea spiral. Shown here is the microscopy cross section through the cochlea turn. Modiolus is the part that runs through the middle.

This is a microCT cross section through the turn this time in different angle. Modiolus is in the middle. Nerves and blood vessels run through the modiolus.

Nerve Fibers: Inside the modiolus, nerve fibers connecting the cochlea to the brain runs through. RIGHT: microscopy image showing nerves. Green round circles are neurons. Noodle-like extensions are nerve fibers.

Lateral wall:
If the center of the cochlear spiral is called the modiolus, outer edges of the spiral is called the “lateral wall. This is a microscopy image of the cross section through the lateral wall. Red and green are different cells that make up the lateral wall. Blue dots are cell nuclei.
